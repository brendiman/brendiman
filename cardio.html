Why Is Cardio Important?

First, let’s define cardio. Cardio is really any continuous movement that gets your heart rate up and puts your lungs into action.
Despite how it may feel in the moment, cardio is good for you. Besides the inherent heart and lung health benefits, cardio exercise also
aids in weight loss, reduces the risk of heart disease, improves bone density, boosts mood, and decreases stress.
The American Heart Association recommends getting at least 30 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity (ie walking) at least
five days a week or at least 25 minutes of vigorous aerobic activity (ie running, cycling, or jumping rope) three days per week.
High-intensity, muscle-building movements, like you would encounter in a circuit training routine, are anaerobic activities.
"Cardio is extremely important. It keeps our hearts healthy and keeps us feeling good overall.
I have so much more positive energy throughout my day when I get in a good sweat," says Chris Brickley,
NBA Skills Trainer and BODYARMOR ambassador. "But it’s important to remember to stay hydrating when practicing cardio.
Prior, during, and after my workouts, I’ll drink BODYARMOR to keep myself in the game."
