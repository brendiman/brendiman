1. Deadlift

You’ll find a whole bunch of gym adrenaline rushed freaks who deadlift. But, mind you,
if you think you can do it at a go, you’ll most probably stay paralysed for life. So before you deadlift,
you ought to master weightlifting. After that, you need learn how to hinge at the hips – no,
it’s not squatting but sitting back. It is when you perfect this position
that you can deadlift (without causing any injury). Make sure you learn from a trainer and not YouTube.
Time: Totally depends on which weightlifting stage you are in.

2. Lat Pulldown

One of the easiest exercises to work your back muscles, indeed. It’s a machine version of a pull-up where you sit on a bench press and pull down a bar tied to weights. This, my friend, you’ll be able to pull off in a second.

Time: 2 sets of 20 reps

Pull-up

Find a high-raised bar. Place both your hands on the bar and lift yourself. That’s it. Since the emphasis is on your biceps, your back is automatically worked on.

Time: 3 sets of 6 reps.
